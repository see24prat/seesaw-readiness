The 5 rules that make this work

  1. The Seesaw soul: you squat and press every session — loads undulate heavy → light → medium so you build without digging a hole.
  2. It follows you, not the clock. Train any day. Finish a workout, tap Done, and it advances to the next — the week ticks up every 3 sessions.
  3. Log it, and it learns. Enter your weight + tap how it felt; next time that lift's target nudges up, holds, or backs off.
  4. Shoulder/elbow-smart. Seesaw dumbbell pressing (free-scapula), pull ≥ push, cuff & forearm care daily. No barbell-bench-every-day, no dips.
  5. Weigh in daily, watch the line. Day-to-day bounces are water — the 2-week trend is the truth.

How that shaped your plan

  • Kept the Seesaw soul: squat + a press every session, loads undulating across the week.
  • Swapped barbell-bench-every-day for the seesaw DB press — your favorite move and Cressey's shoulder-safe choice. Weekly pull is the trap-bar deadlift.
  • Pull ≥ push every session, plus daily band pull-aparts / external rotations / wrist work.
  • One lighter unilateral day (split squats, Boyle) and loaded carries (Dan John).
  • 8–12 rep accessories + a short abs finisher (Schoenfeld) — leanness without wrecking recovery.
Left out on purpose: Boyle's "back squats are unsafe" claim (overstated — we kept them, coached well) and most of Meyers's specifics (brand more than evidence). Burpees and deep dips are out — too rough on your shoulder/elbow.

Only rule: try not to stack two hard days back-to-back. Otherwise, train when you can — 3 sessions banked each week is the win.

Running, the joint-friendly way

  • ~80% of runs easy (Zone 2 — you can hold a conversation). Easy miles burn fat, build your engine, spare your knees.
  • Rusty? Run/walk. e.g. 3 min run / 1 min walk × 5. Add only ~10% time per week.
  • One faster session/week max — only from week 4+ once you've built a base.

Shoulder & elbow playbook

You flagged these — so the whole program is built around keeping them happy (Cressey's playbook).

✅ Lean into
  • Seesaw DB & landmine presses (free scapula)
  • Face pulls + band pull-aparts
  • Band external rotations (cuff)
  • Pull ≥ push, controlled tempo
🚫 Limit / avoid
  • Barbell bench every day
  • Behind-the-neck press, upright rows
  • Deep bar dips, skullcrushers
  • Ego-pressing to failure

Tennis-elbow shield

Wrist-extension eccentrics + hammer curls 2–3×/week (built into Days B & C). Warm the forearm before you serve.

Before you serve (2-min court warm-up)

  • Band pull-aparts ×20
  • External rotations ×12/arm
  • Arm circles + cross-body swings ×10 each
  • A few easy serves before going full power
Pain rule: sharp or pinching pain = stop, switch to a friendlier angle. Dull muscle fatigue = fine, that's the work.

Fuel: gentle deficit, protein-anchored

~1 lb/week — slow enough to keep strength, sustainable with a newborn. ~10–13 lbs in 3 months lands right here.

The four guardrails

  • Protein is the anchor: ~150–180 g/day. A palm of protein each meal + a shake.
  • Modest deficit: ~300–500 cal under maintenance. "Slightly smaller plates," not starvation.
  • Plate template: ½ veg/fruit, ¼ protein, ¼ smart carbs (around lifts & tennis). Don't fear healthy fats.
  • Hydrate: ~3 L water/day, more on tennis days.
Sleep is the wildcard. With a 10-week-old, nights will be broken. Keep the deficit gentle, lean on protein + daily steps, let consistency win.

General guidance, not medical advice — if anything feels off, check with your doctor.
